Biography

Steve Jobs (1955â€“2011) was an American entrepreneur who co-founded Apple Inc., shaping modern technology. In 1976, he established Apple with Steve Wozniak, introducing the Apple I and later the Apple II, revolutionizing personal computing. After leaving Apple in 1985, he founded NeNext Computer and acquired Pixar Animation Studios. His 1997 return revitalized Apple, leading to iconic products like the iPod, iPhone, and iPad that blended technology and design. Jobs' perfectionism and innovative spirit drove Apple's success, though his intense leadership style caused challenges. Diagnosed with pancreatic cancer in 2003, he continued working while confronting mortality. His legacy endures beyond technology. His 2005 Stanford commencement speech emphasized following one's heart and embracing challenges. On October 5, 2011, Jobs passed away, leaving an unparalleled impact. Apple's ongoing achievements and his design ethos ensure his enduring influence. Steve Jobs' journey symbolizes the transformative power of innovation and the significance of approaching problems uniquely.

Timeline

  • February 24, 1955:
    Steve Jobs is born in San Francisco, California.

  • April 1, 1976:
    Jobs, Steve Wozniak, and Ronald Wayne co-found Apple Computer, later known as Apple Inc.

  • April 11, 1976:
    Apple I, their first product, is introduced at the Homebrew Computer Club.

  • January 3, 1977:
    Apple Computer is incorporated.

  • June 5, 1977:
    Apple II, a groundbreaking personal computer, is launched.

  • January 19, 1983:
    Apple introduces the Lisa, a commercial computer with a graphical user interface.

  • anuary 24, 1984:
    Apple releases the Macintosh, a user-friendly personal computer with a graphical interface.

  • September 17, 1985:
    Jobs resigns from Apple following internal conflicts.

  • October 12, 1988:
    Jobs founds NeXT Computer, a company focused on advanced workstations.

  • 1986:
    Jobs acquires Pixar Animation Studios from Lucasfilm.

  • November 22, 1995:
    Pixar releases "Toy Story," the world's first feature-length computer-animated film.

  • 1996:
    Apple acquires NeXT Computer, marking Jobs' return to Apple.

  • September 23, 1997:
    Jobs becomes Apple's interim CEO.

  • May 6, 1998:
    The iMac, a revolutionary all-in-one computer, is introduced.

  • October 23, 2001:
    The iPod, a portable digital music player, is unveiled.

  • August 15, 2002:
    Apple introduces the iMac G4, known for its unique design.

  • June 29, 2007:
    The first iPhone is released, transforming the smartphone industry.

  • January 27, 2010:
    Apple unveils the iPad, popularizing tablet computing.

  • August 24, 2011:
    Jobs resigns as Apple's CEO due to health issues.

  • October 5, 2011:
    Steve Jobs passes away at the age of 56.
